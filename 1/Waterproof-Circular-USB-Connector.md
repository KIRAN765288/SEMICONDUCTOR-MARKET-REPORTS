Key Features:


Waterproofing Standard:
These connectors are often rated based on international standards like IP (Ingress Protection) ratings. A common rating for waterproof USB connectors is IP67 or IP68, where the first digit (6) represents dust-tight protection, and the second digit (7 or 8) represents water resistance (7 for immersion up to 1 meter, and 8 for prolonged immersion).

Circular Shape:
The connector has a circular form factor, which is common in industrial and outdoor applications due to its robustness and ability to provide better sealing compared to other shapes.

Material and Sealing:
The connectors are typically made of high-grade materials like stainless steel, brass, or plastic, with rubber or silicone seals that prevent water ingress.
USB Compatibility:
The connector supports standard USB protocols, such as USB 2.0, USB 3.0, or even newer versions, depending on the application. The internal electrical connections are similar to typical USB connectors, providing data transfer and power.

Locking Mechanism:
Many waterproof circular USB connectors feature a threaded or bayonet-style locking mechanism to ensure a tight and secure connection, further enhancing water resistance.

Applications:
These connectors are widely used in industries such as marine, military, outdoor electronics, automotive, and industrial automation, where reliable connectivity in wet or humid conditions is critical.

Durability:

Designed to withstand harsh conditions, including vibration, dust, dirt, and moisture, these connectors are often rugged and durable.
